1.Nike Air Max

  • Brand: Nike

  • Purpose: Lifestyle & running shoes

  • Signature Feature: Visible Air cushioning in the sole (air bubble that you can see)

  • Style: Trendy, casual, often worn for fashion and comfort

  • History: First released in 1987 (Air Max 1) with revolutionary visible Air technology

  • Typical Use: Everyday wear, light running, street fashion

  • Example Models: Air Max 90, Air Max 97, Air Max 270

    • Summary: Air Max is all about comfort and style, with that iconic visible Air unit.
    It’s less about basketball or sports performance now, more about casual and streetwear fashion.

2.Air Jordan

  • Brand: Nike (Michael Jordan signature line)

  • Purpose: Basketball & lifestyle

  • Signature Feature: Basketball-focused performance, often with high-top design, Jumpman logo

  • Style: Sporty and iconic—lots of retro releases, highly collectible

  • History: First released in 1985 (Air Jordan 1), designed for basketball legend Michael Jordan

  • Typical Use: Basketball performance or fashion/streetwear

  • Example Models: Air Jordan 1, Air Jordan 3, Air Jordan 11

    • Summary: Air Max is all about comfort and style, with that iconic visible Air unit.
    It’s less about basketball or sports performance now, more about casual and streetwear fashion.

3.New Balance

  • Brand: New Balance (American company, not Nike)

  • Purpose: Running, walking, and casual lifestyle

  • Signature Feature: Emphasis on comfort, stability, and wide sizing options

  • Style: Simple, classic, sometimes retro-looking

  • History: Founded in 1906, started as a running shoe company, known for quality and comfort

  • Typical Use: Running, walking, work, everyday casual wear

  • Example Models: 990 series, 574, 1080

    • Summary: New Balance is all about comfort, support, and classic design. Less hype/fashion-focused than Nike, but very popular for practical, long-wear sneakers.
    New Balance is all about comfort and practicality first, with style as a bonus—unlike Air Max (fashion-focused) or Air Jordan (basketball + hype).
    New Balance is a sneaker brand from the USA that focuses on comfort, support.
